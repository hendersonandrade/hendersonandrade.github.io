Hybrid connectivity: VPN and ExpressRoute

Hub-Spoke and Virtual WAN define the topology inside Azure; hybrid connectivity defines how on-premises gets there. The two options: VPN Gateway (an encrypted tunnel over the Internet) and ExpressRoute (a private connection via a provider). This is Part 4 of the series — they are components that plug into the models from Parts 2 and 3.

Henderson Andrade Published Jun 19, 2026 ~14 min read Azure · Hybrid · VPN · ExpressRoute
VPN Gateway ExpressRoute BGP · IPsec Series · 4/6

Executive summary

ℹ
VPN uses the public Internet with IPsec encryption — quick to deploy, cheap, ideal for smaller environments, simple branches, contingency and labs. ExpressRoute is a private connection via a provider — predictable, suited to critical traffic, high volume and datacenter migration. The most common enterprise pattern combines both: ExpressRoute as the primary path and VPN as backup. Neither replaces a firewall, application encryption or identity control — connectivity is not security.
⚠
Gateway SKU throughput, redundancy options, SLA and ExpressRoute availability per region/provider vary and change — validate against the official documentation and with the provider before sizing.

VPN Gateway: Site-to-Site and Point-to-Site

The VPN Gateway creates encrypted tunnels between Azure and external networks over the Internet. Two modes:

  • Site-to-Site (S2S) — connects an entire on-premises network to Azure over an IPsec/IKE tunnel, terminating on a compatible local VPN device. Can use BGP. Serves as primary connectivity for smaller environments or as an ExpressRoute backup.
  • Point-to-Site (P2S) — connects individual users to the VNet (remote access, administration). Can be complemented or replaced by modern/Zero Trust access solutions, depending on the case.

ExpressRoute

ExpressRoute establishes private connectivity between your infrastructure (via a connectivity provider) and the Microsoft Cloud — traffic does not traverse the public Internet. The core concepts:

  • ExpressRoute circuit — the unit of connectivity, provisioned with a provider.
  • Private peering — the BGP peering that connects your VNets (via the ExpressRoute Gateway).
  • ExpressRoute Gateway — the gateway in the (hub) VNet that terminates the circuit.
  • Global Reach — connects on-premises sites to each other through the Microsoft backbone.
  • Hub-Spoke or Virtual WAN — the ExpressRoute Gateway lives in the traditional hub (Part 2) or the Virtual Hub (Part 3).

BGP and route exchange

Both VPN (with BGP enabled) and ExpressRoute use BGP to advertise and learn prefixes dynamically between Azure and on-premises. This simplifies routing and enables failover, but it requires care: incorrect advertisements or too many prefixes can cause unexpected routes or exceed limits. Practice summarization and prefix control, and always validate the effective routes (Part 1).

High availability and backup

  • VPN as primary — use redundant tunnels and BGP for smaller environments.
  • ExpressRoute as primary — evaluate circuit redundancy and, per continuity requirements, S2S VPN as a backup path.
  • Failover — BGP must be designed to reconverge onto the backup path without asymmetric routing.

VPN vs. ExpressRoute

CriterionVPN GatewayExpressRoute
TransportPublic Internet (IPsec)Private connection via provider
PrivacyEncrypted over the InternetDoes not use the public Internet
PredictabilityDepends on the InternetHigher predictability
CostGenerally lowerGenerally higher (circuit + provider)
DeploymentRelatively fastCan take longer (provider)
Best forSmaller environments, simple branches, backup, labCritical, high volume, DC migration
HA / backupRedundant tunnelsOften with a VPN contingency

VPN is not "always inferior": for many scenarios it fully suffices. And ExpressRoute does not replace a firewall or application encryption — it is transport, not security.

Checklist

  • Requirement — Does the case need private/predictable connectivity (ExpressRoute) or does VPN suffice?
  • Continuity — Is there a backup (VPN) when ExpressRoute is critical?
  • BGP — Prefixes summarized and controlled, with no risk of incorrect advertisement?
  • Gateway — ExpressRoute/VPN Gateway in the hub (Part 2) or Virtual Hub (Part 3), with gateway transit for spokes?
  • Security — Connectivity not confused with security: firewall and identity in place?
